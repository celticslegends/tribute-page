Patrick Henry

GIVE ME LIBERTY OR GIVE ME DEATH!

-Henry was a leader of the anti-federalists and helped gain the adoption of the Bill of Rights.

Here is a timeline of Patrick Henry's life and his contributions to America:

  • 1736 - Patrick Henry was born at Studley in Hanover County, Virginia.
  • 1748 - Henry witnessed the preaching of Samuel Davies and became enthused with speaking during the "Great Awakening".
  • 1752 - His father helped him open a store, but because of his laziness the business eventually failed.
  • 1754 - Patrick Henry married Sarah Shelton and became a landowner and farmer.
  • 1760 - The tobacco farm that he and his wife managed failed. Henry then studies to become a lawyer and he opens his own law firm in Williamsburg.
  • 1764 - Henry found success as a lawyer, and within a few years had so much business that people began to take notice of him.
    • 1765 - On May 29, Henry delivers his famous 'Caesar-Brutus Speech' "If this be treason, make the most of it!" in the debate against his resolutions against the Stamp Act. When Henry compared George III to Julius Caesar and King Charles I, he was accused of treason.
  • 1768 - October 1: English war ships sail into Boston Harbor.
  • 1769 - Patrick Henry is admitted into the bar of the General Court.
  • 1771 - Henry helps create the Virginia Committee of Correspondence. The committees of correspondence were shadow governments organized by the Patriots leaders of the 13 colonies.
  • 1775 - March 23, Patrick Henry delivered his famous speech: Give me liberty, or give me death!"
  • 1775 - July 6: Declaration on the Causes and Necessity of Taking Up Arms stating that Americans are "resolved to die as free men rather than live as slaves."
  • 1775 - August 5: Patrick Henry elected commander-in-chief of Virginia's military forces.
  • 1776 - June 29: Patrick Henry is elected first governor of the Commonwealth of Virginia and is elected 5 terms. He helps write the Virginia Constitution, as well as the Virginia Decleration of Rights.
  • 1788 - June: Convention begins debate over the Constitution. As a leader of the anti-federalists, Patrick Henry's opposition of too much central power fuels movement for a Bill of Rights.
  • 1791 - December 15: Ten amendments ratified as Federal Bill of Rights.
  • 1799 - March 4: Patrick Henry's final speech at Charlotte Courthouse.
  • 1799 - June 6: The death of Patrick Henry at Red Hill.

"Guard with jealous attention the public liberty. Suspect everyone who approaches that jewel. Unfortunately, nothing will preserve it but downright force. Whenever you give up that force, you are inevitably ruined."

Patrick Henry
